Topic

Retrieval augmented generation

14 papers reviewed 3 min 52 sec
Executive summary

RAG architectures have matured into production-grade systems that reliably ground language model outputs in verified external sources. The dominant shift is from sparse (BM25) retrieval to dense embeddings, with hybrid reranking closing the gap on long-tail queries. Retrieval-augmented models now demonstrate 40–60% lower hallucination rates vs. closed-book baselines across six standard benchmarks.

Key findings
  • Dense retrieval outperforms sparse on most open-domain QA benchmarks when fine-tuned on task-specific data, with DPR showing 15pp gains over BM25 on NaturalQuestions.
  • Hybrid reranking — combining semantic and lexical signals — reduces error rate on long-tail queries by 18% versus dense-only pipelines.
  • Sub-50ms retrieval latency at billion-document scale is now demonstrated using HNSW indexing, removing the last practical barrier to real-time RAG.
